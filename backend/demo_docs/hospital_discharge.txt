HOSPITAL COURSE
================================================================================

Mr. Vance underwent Doppler ultrasound on admission, which confirmed an
occlusive thrombus in the left popliteal vein. He was started on IV heparin
and transitioned to oral Warfarin therapy for long-term anticoagulation.
Blood pressure was monitored closely throughout admission and remained
controlled on his existing Lisinopril regimen.

His shortness of breath resolved by day 2 and CT pulmonary angiography was
negative for pulmonary embolism. He was ambulatory with compression stockings
by day 4 and cleared for discharge on day 5.

DISCHARGE MEDICATIONS

CONTINUE the following medications:

1. WARFARIN (Coumadin) — 5 mg tablet
   - Take ONE tablet by mouth ONCE DAILY at the same time every evening.
   - Do NOT skip doses. If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember

   - Continue as previously prescribed for blood pressure management.

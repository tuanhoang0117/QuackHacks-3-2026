DIAGNOSIS & PROCEDURE NOTES
================================================================================

Mr. Vance presented on March 18, 2024 with a 5-day history of severe
right lower jaw pain, swelling of the gum, and sensitivity to temperature.
Clinical examination and periapical X-ray confirmed a periapical abscess
at tooth #30 (lower right first molar).

Procedure performed under local anesthetic (lidocaine, latex-free equipment
used — patient has documented latex allergy). The abscess was incised and
drained. Area irrigated with saline. Patient tolerated procedure well.

A course of antibiotics has been prescribed to clear the remaining bacterial
infection. Root canal therapy or extraction will be discussed at follow-up
depending on patient preference and tooth viability.

NOTE TO PATIENT: Please inform ALL other treating physicians and pharmacists
that you have been prescribed Metronidazole. If you are currently taking any
blood thinners or other medications, contact your doctor before taking this
antibiotic.

================================================================================
PRESCRIBED MEDICATIONS
================================================================================

1. METRONIDAZOLE (Flagyl) — 500 mg tablet
   - Take ONE tablet by mouth TWICE DAILY (every 12 hours).
   - Take for a FULL 7 DAYS — do not stop early even if you feel better.
   - Take with food to reduce stomach upset.
   - DO NOT DRINK ALCOHOL during treatment and for 48 hours after your
     last dose. Combining alcohol with Metronidazole causes severe nausea,
     vomiting, and flushing.
   - Common side effects: metallic taste in the mouth, mild nausea,
     headache. These are normal and temporary.
   - If you experience numbness or tingling in your hands or feet, or
     seizures, stop taking and contact us immediately.

   *** IMPORTANT: If you are taking Warfarin or any other blood thinner,
   contact your prescribing physician BEFORE starting this antibiotic.
   Metronidazole can increase the effect of blood thinners significantly. ***

================================================================================
POST-PROCEDURE CARE INSTRUCTIONS
================================================================================

PAIN MANAGEMENT:
  - For mild pain: Acetaminophen (Tylenol) 500–1000 mg every 6 hours
    as needed. Do NOT exceed 3,000 mg per day.
  - DO NOT take ibuprofen (Advil) or aspirin — these increase bleeding
    risk, especially if you are on blood thinners.

ORAL HYGIENE:
  - Do NOT rinse vigorously for 24 hours after the procedure.
  - After 24 hours: rinse gently with warm salt water (1/2 tsp salt in
    8 oz warm water) after meals and before bed.
  - Brush teeth gently. Avoid the treated area for the first 48 hours.

DIET:
  - Soft foods only for the first 48 hours (yogurt, soup, mashed foods).
  - Avoid very hot or very cold foods and drinks near the treated tooth.
  - Avoid chewing on the right side until cleared at follow-up.

SWELLING:
  - Some swelling and tenderness is normal for 2–3 days.
  - Apply a cold compress (20 min on, 20 min off) for the first 24 hours.
